Summer time spring cleaning! Time to look over the insp rules and make them a heck of a lot simpler and closer to the 5e RAW! Sorry Hillfolk!

I already posted this on my blog and our internal campaign website but here I\u2019ll add a bonus chapter before the text starts proper; the what was I thinking design rationale.

So what do I like about insp, what do I want to get out of it, what works about it, what is extraneous or muddy?

  1. I like the \u201cyou have a resource to spend before the roll\u201d / Fortune at the End \u201cbetting\u201d/\u201cinvesting\u201d, demonstrates that you care more about certain rolls
  2. I like how the character\u2019s traits and relationships become relevant on the dice layer
  3. I like how you are rewarded for \u201cgiving in\u201d. It lets the conversations flow naturally instead of being resolved by a charisma check or w/e.
  4. I like flashlight dropping obv
  5. I didn\u2019t like having to deal with a clunky econ that just breaks down and either chokes or floods. I didn\u2019t like it with Fate, I don\u2019t like it with Hillfolk.
  6. I didn\u2019t like the uncrisp phrasing of how much can you get from a 2-token spend force. \u201cNot necessarily what they asked for but a significant emotional concession\u201d uh\u2026
  7. I didn\u2019t like the super confusing \u201cyou pay one but you pay from the bank if you don\u2019t have one but you can only do that for some particular types of spends\u201d; that confusing & unusual interaction happing in tight conjunction with the already confusing \u201cit\u2019s the loser who wins the insp\u201d made it hopeless to remember & learn

So. What has changed.

Economy: gone. Back to what I loved about 5e insp in the first place; it\u2019s a binary predicate state. You have inspiration or you don\u2019t have inspiration. We still use tokens; it reinforces that you can get inspiration even if you already have it. Then as you spend it, you lose it all.

Hillfolky spends: gone. I realized that can keep the same reward state that I wanted since the tokens in 2097e are useful in the procedural layer, unlike Hillfolk proper where procedural tokens and drama tokens are completely separate worlds.

\u201cYou pay one\u201d: gone. You always get insp from the bank. The person who gives in gets the insp, just as in Hillfolk. As confusing as that rule is, I\u2019ve kept it. That\u2019s who I want to reward.

Flashlight dropping: explicitly rewarded. However, the idea on here that the other people should get the insp was still interesting. And that also is kind of a consequence of another rule that I now made more implicit: if your party member starts flashlight dropping you can challenge them in conversation. And either you get their insp (if you give in) or they\u2019ll lose the trait. So it\u2019s win/win for you.

Inspiration

Short summary of how to get it

You gain insp from giving in (in conversations), or for letting your fellow party members down.

First of all, grab an insp token so that the rest of us can see what\u2019s going on, then challenge one of your (or someone elses) relationships, traits, ideals, bonds or flaws through a conversation, through an action, or through an inaction or failed action.

Challenging conversation

  1. Look through either your own or someone elses traits, ideals, bonds or flaws, or at a relationship that one of you has, or the relationship between the two of you.
  2. Grab an insp token and place it between the two of you.
  3. Challenge that trait or relationship through conversation.
  4. The insp token goes to the person who gives in.

Be ready to have to deal with the consequences of giving in. If you give in on your own trait, you might lose the trait.

Through action

Grab an insp token.

Show, or tell, how your trait, ideal, bond or flaw leads you to do something that other player characters rely on you to not do.

If this is challenged it can lead to a conversation. In that case, the person who gives in gets the inspiration.

Inaction or failed action

Grab an insp token.

Show, or tell, how your trait, ideal, bond or flaw prevents you from succeeding on something that other player characters rely on you to do.

Sometimes it\u2019s you trying to do the thing but failing (for example you have the flaw \u201cI do sloppy work\u201d). If you already rolled a die, flip the successful d20 to %%1%% and fail. (You don\u2019t gain inspiration if you aready failed on the roll.) If there weren\u2019t a die roll for this particular task, just time spent, then the time is wasted.

Sometimes you just don\u2019t roll. This can be you trying & failing (\u201cI do sloppy work\u201d) or it can be you not even trying (\u201cI refuse to polish the armor of those beneath me\u201d).

If this is challenged it can lead to a conversation. In that case, the person who gives in gets the inspiration.

Who can have it?

Player characters can have insp. NPC insp goes to the DM. So inspiration that a beloved NPC ally of the players get, can be used by the evil hobgoblins against the players.

Using It!

If you have inspiration, you can spend it to impose advantage or disadvantage on any d20 roll. (If you have more than one token, spend them all.)

Keeping It!

You don\u2019t need to keep track of the exact amount of insp tokens you have. You either have inspiration or you don\u2019t. If you end the session with inspiration, you keep it until you use it.
