Keith Johnstone in 'Impro' said:

I started my work on narrative by trying to make the improvisers conscious of the implications of the scenes they played. I felt that an artist ought to be \u2018committed\u2019, and that he should be held responsible for the effects of his work\u2014it seemed only common sense. I got my students to analyse the content of Red Riding Hood and The Sleeping Beauty and Moby Dick and The Birthday Party, but this made them even more inhibited. I didn\u2019t realize that if the people who thought up Red Riding Hood had been aware of the implications, then they might never have written the story. This was at a time when I had no inspiration as a writer at all, but I didn\u2019t twig that the more I tried to understand the \u2018real\u2019 meaning, the less I wrote.
