\n Hi, all! I'm new here, and really excited to see that this forum exists. Clearly I've got a lot of browsing to do.

In the meantime, I was hoping you could help me identify a game in print form -- I acquired it at a game store within the last few years, but my bag was stolen shortly after that, and now I can't seem to google it up despite several attempts.

The most immediately distinctive thing about it is that it was printed on a series of concentric circles; you spin each circle upward to reveal the next page, and the text continues on the other side once you reach the end of the stack. Reading the format conveys the impression of a spiral or loop, though at the end of the day it's just a series of circles bound by a single metal stud. There were maybe 10 double-sided pages in all.

The conceptual approach was similar to a collaborative storyboarder like Microscope, but it seemed more visual in nature. From what little I can remember, the first player uses a large sheet of paper to write down an event, and then subsequent players write other events and use colored pens or pencils to trace lines that show the connecting factors between a newly created event and any previously created ones.

Strike any bells? I found it on a shelf at Endgame in Oakland -- which, appropriately given the name, is sadly now closed -- and my email to the shop earlier last year didn't receive a response. From the research I've already done, it doesn't appear to be Continuum or Timestream, both of which I believe are discussed here.

Thanks in advance!
Chris
