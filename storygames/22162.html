\n A lot of games are clever about deliberately failing rolls \u201cYou need to deliberately fail an empathy check to shoot him\u201d etc.

But if I don\u2019t want to be clever. If I just want to calculate the inverse. To get a \u201chardness\u201d roll derived from your empathy. Or whatever. Because it feels so weird to roll to fail.

Let\u2019s say there\u2019s a +4 mod vs DC 10 chance of making a wisdom save (for dropping a torch without it going out). And I want to flip it to get the chance for dropping a torch and putting it out? For when you want to do that?

Do two things.
The new DC: 22 minus the old DC.
The new mod: 0 minus the old mod.

Or, say, \u201cRoll vs DC 12 but minus your wis save instead of adding it.\u201d (The 12 is 22-10.)

Or if you want to obfuscate & be illusionist.
I mean I don\u2019t see the point. But.
The new DC: 22, minus the old DC, but plus twice the mod.

You need to know the mod (I usually don\u2019t know my PC\u2019s mods) and it becomes contingent on which PC is making the save which is unblorby.

But.

With a +4 wis save mod, the new DC becomes therefore\u2026 20.

22 -10 +4 +4 = 20.

(And also this is yet another reason why I want to switch from d20 + mod vs DC to an ACKS style \u201cthrow\u201d thac10 system. Because to flip any \u201croll-over\u201d roll you just do 22 - it.)
