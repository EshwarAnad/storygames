    \n Jackson's character Bill had every scene end bad for him. In every one of his scenes he tried to stand up to someone, his kid, Dr Crusher, or Captain Amazing, and got shut down or forced to give up his position. Yet he seemed like a truly heroic character in a very realistic way, someone who kept trying to do the best he could despite being utterly unable to get anyone to listen to him. In fact he was the only heroic character in the story. His big happy ending was running a plumbing company and getting re-married, despite losing his son. It's wonderful having a protagonist like that to ground the story among all the overly ambitious madmen.
